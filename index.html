    Founded by local history enthusiasts, the Society’s mission is to collect, protect, and share artifacts, documents, and stories that reflect Martinsville and Henry County’s diverse history. Through exhibits, lectures, and educational programs, it connects present generations with the people and events that shaped the area, from its early frontier days to its industrial growth in furniture and textiles. 

    Visitors can view rotating and permanent exhibits showcasing: Colonial and Revolutionary-era artifacts, including household items and personal effects. Industrial heritage displays highlighting Martinsville’s furniture and textile industries.

     

    Perfect for toasting to good times and great company! 

    The Society partners with local schools and civic groups, offering tailored programs for students and educators. These initiatives help young people appreciate local history and understand its connection to broader American themes. The Martinsville–Henry County Heritage Center & Museum is open year-round with free admission, although donations are welcome.

    By preserving artifacts and sharing stories, the Martinsville–Henry County Historical Society ensures that the region’s cultural identity and collective memory remain vibrant. Whether you are a lifelong resident, a genealogist, or a traveler interested in Virginia history, the Society offers an engaging window into the past and a reminder of the community’s enduring spirit.
